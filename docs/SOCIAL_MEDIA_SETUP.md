## Account Setup Instructions

### Twitter/X Setup

#### Step 1: Create Business Account
1. Go to [twitter.com](https://twitter.com)
2. Click "Create account"
3. Enter business name: **FamilyHub**
4. Use business email: **hello@familyhub.app** (or appropriate domain)
5. Verify phone number
6. Create strong password (16+ characters, mixed case, numbers, symbols)

#### Step 2: Profile Optimization
- **Profile Picture**: FamilyHub logo (400x400px, PNG)
- **Header Image**: Brand banner with warm colors (1500x500px)
- **Bio**: 
  ```
  Where families stay connected, no matter the distance. 💙
  All-in-one family connection platform.
  Download free → [link to app]
  ```
- **Website**: Link to landing page
- **Location**: San Francisco, CA (or HQ location)

#### Step 3: Account Settings
- Enable 2-factor authentication
- Set account to professional/business
- Add business category: "Apps"
- Enable notifications for mentions and replies
- Set up verified badge application (if eligible)

#### Step 4: Initial Actions
- Follow 50-100 relevant accounts (parenting influencers, family wellness, tech)
- Like and retweet 5-10 relevant posts
- Pin first tweet (see Initial Content Posts section)

#### Step 2: Profile Optimization

## Social Media Management Tools

### Recommended Tools

#### Buffer (Scheduling & Analytics)
- **Cost**: $5-$100/month
- **Features**: 
  - Schedule posts across all platforms
  - Analytics and reporting
  - Content calendar
  - Team collaboration
- **Setup**: 
  1. Create Buffer account
  2. Connect all social media accounts
  3. Set up posting schedule
  4. Create content calendar

#### Hootsuite (Comprehensive Management)
- **Cost**: $49-$739/month
- **Features**:
  - Schedule and publish posts
  - Monitor mentions and conversations
  - Analytics and reporting
  - Team management
  - Content calendar
- **Setup**:
  1. Create Hootsuite account
  2. Connect all social media accounts
  3. Set up monitoring streams
  4. Create content calendar

#### Later (Visual Content Planning)
- **Cost**: $15-$80/month
- **Features**:
  - Visual content calendar
  - Schedule posts
  - Analytics
  - User-generated content curation
  - Instagram-focused
- **Setup**:
  1. Create Later account
  2. Connect Instagram and other platforms
  3. Create visual content calendar
  4. Schedule posts

#### Sprout Social (Enterprise)
- **Cost**: $249-$499/month

## Analytics & Tracking

### Key Metrics to Track

#### Engagement Metrics
- **Likes/Reactions**: Track engagement rate
- **Comments**: Monitor conversation and sentiment
- **Shares/Retweets**: Measure content virality
- **Saves**: Identify valuable content
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Track link clicks

#### Reach & Impressions
- **Impressions**: Total times content was seen
- **Reach**: Unique users who saw content
- **Follower Growth**: Track audience growth rate
- **Viral Coefficient**: Measure organic sharing

#### Audience Metrics
- **Follower Demographics**: Age, location, interests
- **Audience Growth Rate**: New followers per week
- **Audience Retention**: Follower churn rate
- **Audience Sentiment**: Positive vs. negative mentions

#### Conversion Metrics
- **App Downloads**: Track from social links
- **Website Traffic**: From social to website
- **Sign-ups**: From social campaigns
- **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**: Cost to acquire user via social

## Crisis Management & Guidelines

### Social Media Guidelines

#### Do's
- ✅ Be authentic and genuine
- ✅ Respond promptly to comments and messages
- ✅ Celebrate user content and stories
- ✅ Share valuable, helpful content
- ✅ Engage with community
- ✅ Be transparent about issues
- ✅ Maintain consistent brand voice
- ✅ Respect user privacy

#### Don'ts
- ❌ Don't engage in arguments or debates
- ❌ Don't delete negative comments (unless spam/harassment)
- ❌ Don't make promises you can't keep
- ❌ Don't share user data without permission
- ❌ Don't post when emotional or angry
- ❌ Don't ignore comments or messages
- ❌ Don't use misleading information
- ❌ Don't spam or over-promote

### Crisis Response Protocol

#### Step 1: Assess the Situation
- Identify the issue
- Determine severity
- Gather all relevant information
- Consult with team

#### Step 2: Respond Quickly
- Acknowledge the issue
- Show empathy
- Provide initial response within 1 hour
- Avoid making excuses

#### Step 3: Take Action
- Investigate the root cause
- Develop solution
- Communicate progress
- Keep stakeholders informed

#### Step 4: Follow Up
- Confirm issue is resolved
- Thank user for bringing it to attention
- Share learnings with team
- Implement preventive measures

### Crisis Response Templates

**Service Issue**
```
We're aware of [issue] and sincerely apologize for the inconvenience. Our team is working on a fix right now. We'll keep you updated and appreciate your patience. In the meantime, please reach out to [support email] if you need immediate assistance.
```

**Negative Review**
```
Thank you for your feedback. We're sorry to hear about your experience. We'd love to make this right. Please reach out to [support email] with details, and we'll work with you to resolve this.
```

**Misinformation**
```
We want to clarify: [correct information]. Thank you for giving us the opportunity to set the record straight. Feel free to reach out if you have any other questions!
```

**Outage/Technical Issue**
```
We're experiencing [issue] and our team is working to resolve it. We'll provide updates every [time interval]. Thank you for your patience and understanding.
```

### Escalation Protocol

**Level 1: Community Manager**
- Standard comments and messages
- General questions
- Positive engagement

**Level 2: Social Media Lead**
- Complaints or issues
- Feature requests
- Sensitive topics

**Level 3: Management**
- Crisis situations
- Media inquiries
- Legal issues
- Major complaints
